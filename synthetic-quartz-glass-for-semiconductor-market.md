<p><p>Synthetic quartz glass for the semiconductor market is primarily produced using two advanced techniques: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Plasma Deposition. The CVD process involves the chemical reaction of gaseous precursors to form a solid, high-purity quartz layer, ensuring excellent thermal and optical properties. In contrast, Plasma Deposition utilizes ionized gases to deposit material onto substrates, enabling precise control over thickness and composition. Both methods are crucial for manufacturing semiconductor components that require superior dielectric and thermal performance.</p></p>
