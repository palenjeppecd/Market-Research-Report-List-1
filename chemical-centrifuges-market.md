<p><p>The chemical centrifuge market features three primary rotor types: swinging-bucket rotors, fixed-angle rotors, and vertical rotors. Swinging-bucket rotors allow samples to hang vertically during rotation, facilitating sedimentation and efficient separation of components. Fixed-angle rotors maintain a constant angle, optimizing separation speed and efficiency for specific applications. Vertical rotors offer a compact design, maximizing centrifuge space while providing rapid separation. Each type caters to distinct laboratory requirements, enhancing sample processing and research outcomes in various chemical and biological disciplines.</p></p>
