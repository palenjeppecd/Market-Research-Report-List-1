<p><p>Acute Ischemic Stroke (AIS) involves the interruption of blood flow to the brain, leading to cell death and impairments in function. Diagnosis typically employs imaging techniques such as CT and MRI to swiftly identify the ischemic area and exclude hemorrhagic strokes. Treatment focuses on restoring blood flow, primarily through intravenous thrombolysis with tissue plasminogen activator (tPA) and mechanical thrombectomy. Additionally, post-stroke care includes rehabilitation therapies aimed at improving patient outcomes.</p><p>The Acute Ischemic Stroke Diagnosis and Treatment Market is expected to experience significant growth, projected at a CAGR of 8.00% during the forecast period. Recent trends showcase a rising adoption of telemedicine in stroke care, enhancing timely diagnosis and treatment. Furthermore, the development of innovative therapeutic options and increased investment in research for neuroprotective agents are reshaping the market landscape. Enhanced awareness of stroke symptoms and improved access to emergency care are also driving market progression, creating a robust environment for diagnostic and therapeutic advancements in AIS management.</p></p>
