<p><p>The Aziridine Crosslinker market is categorized into difunctional and trifunctional groups based on the number of reactive sites. Difunctional aziridine crosslinkers contain two reactive groups, facilitating the formation of crosslinked networks with improved mechanical and thermal properties. In contrast, trifunctional aziridine crosslinkers possess three reactive sites, enabling the creation of more complex networks with enhanced performance characteristics. These crosslinkers are widely used in various applications, including adhesives, coatings, and sealants, due to their versatility and efficiency in polymer formulations.</p></p>
